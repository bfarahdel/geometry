Example 1a

Determine Number and Type of Roots

$x^2 + 6x + 9 = 0$

Repeated Roots

Polynomial equations can have double, triple, or quadruple roots. In general, these are referred to as multiple roots.

Step 1: Factoring

Find the number that, when multiplied by itself equals 9 and added to itself equals 6.

The correct number is 3.
Step 2: Solve for x

The equation in factored form is:

$(x + 3)^2 = 0$

Set the factor to zero: $x + 3 = 0$. What is the value of $x$?

The correct answer is $x = -3$.
Step 3: Graph Location

Based on your answer ($x = -3$), click the exact point on the x-axis where the graph touches the line.

Example 1b

Find All Roots of the Equation

$x^3 + 25x = 0$

f(x) = x³ + 25x

Complex Roots

Complex roots always occur in conjugate pairs. If $a + bi$ is a root, then $a - bi$ is also a root.

Step 1: Factor the Equation

Factor $x$ out of $x^3 + 25x = 0$.

x(x² + ) = 0
The correct expression is $x^2 + 25$.
Step 2: Find the Real Root

From the factored form $x(x^2 + 25) = 0$, set the first factor to zero.

What is the value of the real root?

The real root is $x = 0$.
Step 3: Find the Complex Roots

Set the second factor to zero: $x^2 + 25 = 0$.

Solving: $x^2 = -25$, so $x = \pm\sqrt{-25}$

Select the two imaginary roots (complex conjugates):

$5i$
$-5i$
$3i$
$-3i$
$25i$
$-25i$
$5$
$-5$
The complex roots are $5i$ and $-5i$. These are complex conjugates.
Step 4: Check the Graph

Click the point on the x-axis where the graph crosses. This confirms the real root.

The graph crosses the x-axis at x = 0.
🎯

This equation has one real root ($x = 0$) and two imaginary roots ($5i$ and $-5i$).

Notice that $5i$ and $-5i$ are complex conjugates. Complex conjugate pairs always occur together for polynomial equations with real coefficients.

The graph confirms this: it crosses the x-axis at only one place, $x = 0$.

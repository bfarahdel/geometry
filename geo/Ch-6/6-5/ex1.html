Because \( FGHJ \) is a rhombus, diagonal \( \overline{JG} \) bisects \( \angle FJH \). Find \( m\angle KJH \) given that the whole angle \( m\angle FJH = 82^\circ \).
The diagonals of a rhombus are perpendicular. What is the measure of the angle where they intersect at point \( K \)?
In the right triangle \( \triangle JKH \), the sum of all angles is \( 180^\circ \). Calculate the remaining angle \( m\angle KHJ \):
\( 41 + 90 + m\angle KHJ = 180 \)
By solving the equation, we find \( m\angle KHJ = 49^\circ \). Great job!

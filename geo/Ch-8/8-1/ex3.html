Example 3
Use Geometric Mean with Right Triangles

Find x, y, and z.

Since x is the measure of the altitude drawn to the hypotenuse of right ΔJKL, x is the geometric mean of the lengths of the two segments that make up the hypotenuse, JM and MK.

x=JM·MK
Geometric Mean (Altitude) Theorem
=5·20
Substitution
=100 or 10
Simplify.

Since y is the measure of leg JL, y is the geometric mean of JM, the measure of the segment adjacent to this leg, and JK, the measure of the hypotenuse.

y=JM·JK
Geometric Mean (Leg) Theorem
=5·(20+5)
Substitution
=125 or about 11.2
Use a calculator to simplify.

Since z is the measure of leg KL, z is the geometric mean of MK, the measure of the segment adjacent to KL, and JK, the measure of the hypotenuse.

z=MK·JK
Geometric Mean (Leg) Theorem
=20·(20+5)
Substitution
=500 or about 22.4
Use a calculator to simplify.
